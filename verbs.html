Understanding Russian Verb Forms

When you click on a verb card, you'll find details designed to clarify key aspects of Russian verbs, especially the distinction between actions viewed as ongoing or habitual (imperfective aspect) versus completed or result-oriented (perfective aspect). Featured forms include:

  • Perfective Infinitive: The verb's basic "to..." form that signifies an action completed or achieving a specific result. (e.g., сделать - to do and finish).
  • Unidirectional Infinitives (Imperfective/Perfective): Specifically for verbs of motion (like "to go," "to run"). These show the "to..." forms for movement in a single, specific direction. You'll see both:
    • Imperfective: ongoing one-way motion (e.g., идти - to be going one way)
    • Perfective: a completed one-way trip or the start of one (e.g., пойти - to set off/go one way)
  • Past Tense ("he" form, Imperfective/Perfective): Shows the past tense form for a masculine singular subject ("he did"). This helps illustrate aspectual differences in the past:
    • Imperfective: action in progress, repeated, or a general fact in the past (e.g., он читал - he was reading/used to read).
    • Perfective: action completed in the past (e.g., он прочитал - he read/finished reading).
  • Imperative ("you" singular, Imperfective/Perfective): The command form for telling one person (informal "you," or ты) to do something. Again, shown for both aspects:
    • Imperfective: command to perform an action generally, or to continue it (e.g., читай! - Read!).
    • Perfective: command to complete an action successfully (e.g., прочитай! - Read it all!).

This selection of forms is designed to provide a clear comparison, helping you grasp how Russian verbs change to convey these crucial aspectual meanings. However, please note that not all verbs will have every form listed. Some verbs are primarily used in one aspect, and certain words expressing necessity or obligation (like должен - 'must', or нужно - 'it is necessary') function differently and don't conjugate in this standard way.
